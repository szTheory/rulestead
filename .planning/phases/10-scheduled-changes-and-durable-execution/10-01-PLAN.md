<threat_model>
## Trust Boundaries

| Boundary | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| admin/governance command input -> scheduling contract | Privileged future-dated mutation intent crosses into durable product state here |
| scheduled execution record -> Oban delivery seam | Product-owned state must not be replaced by delivery-substrate state here |

## STRIDE Threat Register

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| T-10-01-01 | T | scheduled-execution schema | mitigate | Store `scheduled_executions` as the source of truth with explicit state checks and unique `idempotency_key`, per D-03 and D-10. |
| T-10-01-02 | R | actor/correlation chain | mitigate | Persist `scheduled_by_*`, approval snapshot, `correlation_id`, and append-only attempt records so submitter/approver/scheduler provenance survives execution, per D-02 and D-12. |
| T-10-01-03 | E | direct scheduling bypass | mitigate | Constrain `execution_mode` to `change_request`, `policy_bypass`, or `emergency_bypass` and do not add generic arbitrary scheduling surfaces, per D-04 and D-06. |
| T-10-01-04 | D | silent workflow expansion | mitigate | Limit action atoms to `publish_ruleset`, `advance_rollout`, `engage_kill_switch`, and `release_kill_switch` and exclude chained/recurring scheduling from the contract, per D-05 through D-07. |
</threat_model>
