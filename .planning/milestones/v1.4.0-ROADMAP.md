## Milestone Summary

**Key Decisions:**

- Prioritized mounted companion proof reclosure over net-new feature work because a broken named proof path was a higher adopter risk than delaying guarded rollout by one milestone.
- Preserved the linked-version, two-package design: `rulestead` remained the runtime/domain contract and `rulestead_admin` remained a mounted companion only.
- Kept the proof surface narrow and scripts-first by centering verification on `RULESTEAD_TEST_SCOPE=mounted_admin_contract bash scripts/ci/test.sh` plus release/support-truth drift suites.
- Treated host-owned auth, policy, actor, and mount prerequisites as explicit contract inputs that must fail closed instead of silently drifting.

**Issues Resolved:**

- The mounted companion boot/runtime contract is now explicit and consistent from generated host fixtures through runtime startup and mounted session behavior.
- The repo-root `mounted_admin_contract` proof bar passes again and maps to one bounded lifecycle and permission contract.
- Mounted proof failures now emit actionable remediation and surface as a stable path-gated CI lane inside `release_gate`.
- Root, package, and maintainer docs now describe the same mounted support surface with exact prerequisite and fallback truth.

**Issues Deferred:**

- `v1.5.0 — Guarded Rollout Foundations` remains the next milestone candidate now that the last materially broken mounted proof surface is shipped.
- `v1.6.0 — Reusable Targeting Deepening` remains queued behind guarded rollout.

**Technical Debt Incurred:**

- No release-blocking technical debt was accepted at close; remaining process debt is limited to future planning and validation hygiene outside the shipped mounted proof scope.
