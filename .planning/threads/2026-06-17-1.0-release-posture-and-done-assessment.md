## Verdict

Rulestead is **~92–95% done for its stated scope. Band: near-done — features are diminishing returns.**
This is repo-verified (lib/ source, 133 test files, runnable demo, 26 guide pages), not phase-counting.
The v1.1–v1.9 feature band is complete; v1.13–v1.18 were already deliberate quality/DX milestones.
Opening another *runtime feature* milestone without a real adopter trigger would be overbuilding by the
project's own discipline.

**The dominant remaining lever is NOT a feature — it is release/version truth + distribution.**

## Key finding — ZeroVer trust-signal mismatch

The published Hex line undersells the platform's actual maturity:

- `rulestead/mix.exs:4` → `@version "0.1.7"`
- `rulestead_admin/mix.exs:4` → `@version "0.1.7"`
- `open_feature_rulestead/mix.exs:4` → `@version "0.1.0"`
- `guides/api_stability.md` ("Versioning Posture") deliberately carries the **v0.1.0 contract forward
  on the `0.1.x` Hex line**, decoupled from internal "v1.0 GA → v1.18" milestone naming.

This is a **deliberate, documented decision — not drift.** But from a serious Phoenix SaaS adopter's
due-diligence lens, a `0.1.7` + "anything not listed may change without notice" badge sits on an
API-frozen, RBAC-complete, governance-rich, battle-tested platform. The version number actively
scares off the exact adopters the product is ready for. Classic ZeroVer.
